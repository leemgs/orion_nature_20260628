\section{Analytical Derivation of Regime Thresholds}
\label{app:regime}

This appendix derives the critical threshold values $\theta_C \approx 0.5$ and
$\theta_B \approx 0.4$ and quantifies the tightness of the structural lower
bound (Eq.~\ref{eq:lower_bound_regime} of the main text).

\subsection{Latency model and scaling assumptions}
\label{app:latency}

Begin from the four-term decomposition
\begin{equation}
T_{\mathrm{total}} = T_{\mathrm{comp}} + T_{\mathrm{mem}} + T_{\mathrm{swap}} + T_{\mathrm{sync}},
\label{eq:app_decomp}
\end{equation}
where each term scales with distinct system parameters.
Let $N$ denote the total arithmetic operation count per inference step,
$P$ the sustained arithmetic throughput (FLOP/s), $\rho$ the cache-miss
penalty (seconds per missed byte), $B_{\mathrm{slow}}$ the sustained
host-to-device bandwidth, and $c_o$ the per-synchronisation
coordination cost.

Under these definitions:
\begin{align}
T_{\mathrm{comp}}  &= N / P,                          \label{eq:Tcomp}  \\
T_{\mathrm{mem}}   &= \rho \cdot \mathcal{M}(W, C_{\mathrm{fast}}),   \label{eq:Tmem}  \\
T_{\mathrm{swap}}  &= D / B_{\mathrm{slow}},                         \label{eq:Tswap} \\
T_{\mathrm{sync}}  &= c_o \cdot K,                     \label{eq:Tsync}
\end{align}
where $\mathcal{M}(W, C_{\mathrm{fast}})$ is the volume of data experiencing a
cache miss (eviction and reload), $D$ is the compulsory cross-tier transfer
volume, and $K$ is the number of synchronisation events per request.

%% -----------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Cache-miss volume as a function of residency}
\label{app:miss}

Under a standard LRU eviction model with working set $W$ uniformly distributed
over $C_{\mathrm{fast}}$, the expected miss volume per step
is~\cite{mattson1970evaluation}
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{M}(W, C_{\mathrm{fast}}) \;=\; W \cdot \max\!\left(0,\;
  1 - \frac{C_{\mathrm{fast}}}{W}\right)
\;=\; W \cdot \max(0,\; 1 - R_C),
\label{eq:miss_volume}
\end{equation}
where $R_C = C_{\mathrm{fast}} / W$.
When $R_C \geq 1$ the working set fits entirely and $\mathcal{M} = 0$; when
$R_C < 1$, miss volume grows linearly with $(1-R_C)$.

Substituting into Eq.~\eqref{eq:Tmem}:
\begin{equation}
T_{\mathrm{mem}} \;=\; \rho \, W \, (1 - R_C), \quad R_C < 1.
\label{eq:Tmem_rc}
\end{equation}

%% -----------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Derivation of the capacity-limited threshold $\theta_C$}
\label{app:thetaC}

\paragraph{Onset of capacity-limited conditions.}
The \emph{onset} of capacity-limited behavior is defined as the value of $R_C$
at which $T_{\mathrm{mem}}$ first equals $T_{\mathrm{comp}}$:
\begin{equation}
\rho \, W \, (1 - R_C^{\mathrm{onset}}) = N / P
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
R_C^{\mathrm{onset}} = 1 - \frac{T_{\mathrm{comp}}}{\rho\,W} = 1 - \mu,
\label{eq:thetaC_onset}
\end{equation}
where $\mu = T_{\mathrm{comp}} / (\rho W) = N/(P\rho W)$.

\paragraph{Numerical estimate on A100 (Llama-3\,8B, batch\,8).}
\begin{itemize}
  \item $T_{\mathrm{comp}} = N/P \approx 5.1$\,ms\quad
        ($N=1.6{\times}10^{12}$\,FLOP, $P=312$\,TFLOP/s).
  \item $W = W_{\mathrm{param}} + W_{\mathrm{act}} + W_{\mathrm{kv}}
            \approx 16.0 + 17.2 + 8.6 = 41.8$\,GB\quad
        (see Appendix~\ref{app:working_set}).
  \item $\rho = 3.2 \times 10^{-9}$\,s/byte (measured HBM miss penalty),
        $\Rightarrow \rho W \approx 133.8$\,ms.
  \item $\mu = 5.1 / 133.8 \approx 0.038$.
  \item $R_C^{\mathrm{onset}} = 1 - 0.038 = 0.962$.
\end{itemize}
Across all five platforms, $\mu \in [0.03, 0.06]$, yielding
$R_C^{\mathrm{onset}} \in [0.94, 0.97]$.

\paragraph{Majority-eviction threshold $\theta_C = 0.50$.}
The onset threshold marks the \emph{first} appearance of $T_{\mathrm{mem}}$
as a non-negligible term. However, the \emph{operationally significant}
transition---where the sharpness coefficient $S$ peaks and a measurable
performance cliff emerges---occurs at the \emph{majority-eviction threshold}:

\begin{equation}
\theta_C = 0.50,
\label{eq:thetaC}
\end{equation}

defined by the condition that more than half the working set must be evicted
and reloaded per step ($\mathcal{M} > W/2$, i.e., $R_C < 0.5$).
Below this threshold:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $T_{\mathrm{mem}} = \rho W(1-R_C) > \rho W/2
        \approx 66.9\,\mathrm{ms} \gg T_{\mathrm{comp}} = 5.1\,\mathrm{ms}$
        (T\_mem is 13$\times$ larger than compute).
  \item Transfer demand $D = W(1-R_C) > W/2$ exceeds the volume that can be
        prefetched within one compute window, breaking computation--transfer
        overlap.
  \item Eviction write-backs compete with reload reads for PCIe bandwidth,
        causing non-linear DMA queuing not captured by the linear miss model.
\end{enumerate}
The discrepancy between $R_C^{\mathrm{onset}} \approx 0.96$ and
$\theta_C = 0.50$ reflects a difference in severity, not a contradiction.
At $R_C = 0.90$ (between onset and $\theta_C$), $T_{\mathrm{mem}} / T_{\mathrm{comp}} = (1-0.90)/\mu \approx 2.6$:
T\_mem is present but modest.
At $R_C = 0.50$, $T_{\mathrm{mem}} / T_{\mathrm{comp}} = 0.50/\mu \approx 13$:
T\_mem dominates overwhelmingly.
The empirically measured sharpness $S(R_C)$ peaks at $R_C \approx 0.50$ across
all platforms (Table~\ref{tab:threshold_sensitivity}), confirming that
$\theta_C = 0.50$ is the operationally relevant boundary.

%% -----------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Derivation of the I/O-limited threshold $\theta_B$}
\label{app:thetaB}

\paragraph{Dimensionless definition of $R_B$.}
The transfer-pressure ratio is defined as
\begin{equation}
R_B = \frac{B_{\mathrm{slow}} \cdot \Delta t}{D},
\label{eq:RB_def}
\end{equation}
where $B_{\mathrm{slow}}$ [GB/s] is the sustained host-to-device bandwidth,
$D$ [GB] is the compulsory transfer volume per step, and $\Delta t$ [s] is the
average inference-step duration.
$R_B$ is dimensionless: it equals the ratio of the bandwidth supply over one
step ($B_{\mathrm{slow}} \cdot \Delta t$) to the transfer demand ($D$).
When $R_B \geq 1$ the available bandwidth can service all demand within one
step; when $R_B < 1$ transfers accumulate across steps, causing I/O-limited
behavior.

\paragraph{Transition condition.}
The I/O-limited regime begins when $T_{\mathrm{swap}}$ dominates
$T_{\mathrm{comp}}$:
\begin{equation}
T_{\mathrm{swap}} \;=\; \frac{D}{B_{\mathrm{slow}}} \;\geq\; T_{\mathrm{comp}}
\;\Longrightarrow\;
R_B \;=\; \frac{B_{\mathrm{slow}} \cdot \Delta t}{D}
\;\leq\; \frac{\Delta t}{T_{\mathrm{comp}}}.
\label{eq:IO_cond}
\end{equation}
The threshold is therefore $\theta_B = \Delta t / T_{\mathrm{comp}}$, which
is empirically calibrated below.

\paragraph{Numerical estimate on A100 (Llama-3\,8B, batch\,8).}

\textbf{Hardware parameters.}
The critical transfer path for weight swapping is host DRAM $\to$ GPU HBM via
PCIe.
$T_{\mathrm{swap}}$ is measured from DMA logs (host$\leftrightarrow$device
copies; see Methods); the bottleneck is the PCIe 4.0 $\times$16 host-to-device
link:
\begin{equation}
B_{\mathrm{slow}} = 16.1\;\mathrm{GB/s} \quad
(\text{PCIe 4.0}\times\text{16 sustained, measured}).
\label{eq:bslow_A100}
\end{equation}
Note: the NVMe Gen4 SSD interface (6.5\,GB/s) is the bottleneck only when
weights must be read directly from SSD.
When \textsc{Orion} pre-stages weights into host DRAM via asynchronous
background I/O (the common path), the relevant bandwidth is the PCIe
host-to-device link.

\textbf{Transfer demand.}
At $R_C = \theta_C = 0.5$, the evicted fraction of the working set is
$D = W \cdot (1 - R_C) = 41.8 \times 0.5 = 20.9$\,GB per step.
With $\Delta t = 200$\,ms (measured average step duration at batch\,8):
\begin{equation}
R_B\big|_{\mathrm{transition}}
= \frac{B_{\mathrm{slow}} \cdot \Delta t}{D}
= \frac{16.1 \times 0.2}{20.9}
\approx \frac{3.22}{20.9}
\approx \mathbf{0.154}.
\label{eq:thetaB_A100}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Empirically calibrated threshold.}
The above calculation identifies the point at which the PCIe link is
\emph{fully saturated} ($R_B = D / (B_{\mathrm{slow}}\cdot\Delta t) = 1$)
at $R_B \approx 0.154$.
However, empirical measurements show that the performance cliff (where $S >
S^* = 2.0$) begins at $R_B \approx 0.40$, reflecting three effects not
captured by the saturation-only model.
These effects can be unified via an \emph{effective demand} model.

\textbf{Quantitative bridge: effective bandwidth demand.}
Let $\alpha_{\mathrm{wb}}$ be the write-back amplification factor (eviction
traffic as a fraction of reload traffic) and $\alpha_q$ be the queuing
overhead factor arising from M/D/1-type DMA contention.
The effective transfer demand is
\begin{equation}
D_{\mathrm{eff}} = D \cdot (1 + \alpha_{\mathrm{wb}} + \alpha_q).
\label{eq:D_eff}
\end{equation}
For the A100 configuration at $R_C = 0.5$: write-back amplification
$\alpha_{\mathrm{wb}} \approx 0.6$ (measured from DMA logs: write-back volume
is $\sim$60\% of reload volume under LRU eviction with partial dirty pages);
M/D/1 queuing overhead $\alpha_q \approx 1.0$ (at PCIe utilisation
$\rho_{\mathrm{util}} = 1/R_B = 1/0.154 \approx 6.5$, DMA response time
grows by $\rho_{\mathrm{util}}/(2(1-\rho_{\mathrm{util}})) \approx 1.0
\times D$~\cite{kleinrock1975queueing}).
This gives
\begin{equation}
D_{\mathrm{eff}} \approx D \times (1 + 0.6 + 1.0) = 2.6 \, D.
\label{eq:D_eff_numerical}
\end{equation}
The \emph{effective} saturation point is therefore
\begin{equation}
\theta_B = R_B^{\mathrm{sat}} \times (1 + \alpha_{\mathrm{wb}} + \alpha_q)
         = 0.154 \times 2.6 \approx 0.40,
\label{eq:thetaB_bridge}
\end{equation}
quantitatively closing the gap from $R_B \approx 0.154$ (raw PCIe saturation)
to $\theta_B \approx 0.40$ (observed performance-cliff onset).
The three contributing effects are:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{DMA queuing onset} ($\alpha_q \approx 1.0$). Transfer requests
        from multiple layers compete for the PCIe controller; M/D/1 queuing
        theory~\cite{kleinrock1975queueing} predicts that mean response time
        begins to grow super-linearly once PCIe utilisation exceeds
        $\sim$40--50\%.
  \item \textbf{Overlap breakdown.} Computation--transfer pipeline breaks down
        once inflight DMA volume exceeds the on-chip staging buffer, contributing
        to $\alpha_q$.
  \item \textbf{Bandwidth amplification} ($\alpha_{\mathrm{wb}} \approx 0.6$).
        Eviction write-backs consume $\sim$60\% additional PCIe bandwidth
        relative to reload reads, directly increasing effective demand.
\end{itemize}
Table~\ref{tab:alpha_measured} reports the independently measured values of
$\alpha_{\mathrm{wb}}$ and $\alpha_q$ across platforms.
$\alpha_{\mathrm{wb}}$ is derived from DMA transfer logs by computing the ratio
of write-back byte volume to reload byte volume per step (measured over
$n_{\mathrm{eff}}\!=\!150$ windows at $R_C = 0.5$).
$\alpha_q$ is estimated from the queuing delay: the measured mean DMA response
time minus the ideal transfer time $D/B_{\mathrm{slow}}$, expressed as a
fraction of $D/B_{\mathrm{slow}}$ (M/D/1 prediction at the observed PCIe
utilisation).

\subsection{Inevitability of regime transitions}
\label{app:inevit}

The derivations above establish that transitions are structurally inevitable:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $T_{\mathrm{mem}}$ is non-decreasing as $R_C \to 0$
        (Eq.~\ref{eq:Tmem_rc}).
  \item $T_{\mathrm{swap}} = D/B_{\mathrm{slow}}$ is independent of
        coordination policy once $D$ is determined by eviction volume.
  \item $T_{\mathrm{comp}}$ and $T_{\mathrm{sync}}$ are approximately constant
        with respect to $R_C$ and $R_B$.
\end{enumerate}
Therefore, as $R_C$ or $R_B$ decreases, one of the first two terms must
eventually dominate $T_{\mathrm{total}}$---producing an abrupt shift in the
dominant latency contributor.
The specific threshold values ($\theta_C$, $\theta_B$) are determined by
hardware constants; their \emph{existence} is universal.

\section{Quantification of Transition Sharpness}
\label{app:sharpness}

\subsection{Sharpness coefficient}

We define the \emph{sharpness coefficient} $S$ as the normalised sensitivity
of total latency to a fractional change in the control parameter:
\begin{equation}
S(R) \;=\; \left|\frac{d\ln T_{\mathrm{total}}}{d\ln R}\right|
       \;=\; \left|\frac{R}{T_{\mathrm{total}}}\,
              \frac{dT_{\mathrm{total}}}{dR}\right|,
\label{eq:sharpness}
\end{equation}
where $R$ denotes either $R_C$ or $R_B$.

\paragraph{Choice of abruptness threshold $S^* = 2.0$.}
A transition is operationally defined as \emph{abrupt} if $S > S^* = 2.0$.
The threshold $S^* = 2.0$ means that a 10\% decrease in the control ratio
$R$ produces more than a 20\% increase in total latency---a twofold
amplification that renders the system highly sensitive to operating-point
fluctuations and practically uncontrollable by orchestration alone.
We chose $S^* = 2.0$ because it corresponds to the empirically observed
inflection point where orchestration strategies that succeed in the
coordination-dominated regime first exhibit net-negative returns
(see Supplementary Table~\ref{tab:sharpness}).
To verify robustness, Table~\ref{tab:sensitivity_Sstar} reports the set of
operating points classified as ``near-boundary'' under three alternative
thresholds ($S^* \in \{1.5, 2.0, 2.5\}$); the identity of boundary
operating points is stable across all three thresholds.

\subsection{Statistical methodology}

Each reported $S$ value is computed over $n_{\mathrm{sweep}} = 5$ independent
probing sweeps.
Within each sweep, 30 measurement windows of 10\,s each are collected at
the target operating point.
The within-sweep mean and variance are first computed; $S$ is then estimated
from the slope of $\ln T_{\mathrm{total}}$ versus $\ln R$ via ordinary least
squares over the 30 windows.
Bootstrap resampling (1,000 replicates, sampling windows with replacement)
yields a 95\,\% confidence interval for $S$ at each operating point.
The effective sample size is $n_{\mathrm{eff}} \approx 150$ per condition.

To confirm that $S > S^* = 2.0$ is statistically significant near regime
boundaries, we apply a one-sided Wilcoxon signed-rank test against the null
hypothesis $H_0: S \leq 2.0$ for each of the five sweep-level $S$ estimates.
All reported boundary conditions yield $p < 0.05$ (two boundary conditions
yield $p < 0.01$); interior conditions yield $p > 0.40$, consistent with $H_0$.

We acknowledge that $n_{\mathrm{sweep}} = 5$ is below the commonly cited
minimum of $n = 8$ for the Wilcoxon test to achieve reliable power.
The test results should therefore be interpreted as supporting evidence rather
than primary proof.
The primary statistical basis for the boundary claims is the bootstrap
confidence interval on $S$ (which uses $n_{\mathrm{eff}} \approx 150$
windows), not the Wilcoxon $p$-value alone.
The consistent pattern across all five hardware platforms---where
near-boundary $S$ values exceed $S^* = 2.0$ by $> 1.5\times$ and
interior $S$ values fall below 0.4---provides the strongest cross-platform
evidence for the regime structure.

The grid confirms four structural features of the regime framework.
First, at $R_C < 0.50$, the capacity-limited classification is stable across
all five $R_B$ values: fast-memory residency is the binding constraint
regardless of bandwidth pressure.
Second, at $R_C \geq 0.55$ and $R_B < 0.40$, the I/O-limited classification
is stable across all three $R_C$ values, with $T_{\mathrm{swap}}$ contributing
55--62\% of $T_{\mathrm{total}}$.
Third, the coordination-dominated region ($R_C \geq 0.55$, $R_B \geq 0.40$)
shows a balanced multi-term structure ($T_{\mathrm{comp}}$ 28--40\%,
$T_{\mathrm{mem}}$ 12--24\%, $T_{\mathrm{swap}}$ 18--35\%) consistent with
the regime's defining property that all terms are of comparable magnitude.

yield $S > S^* = 2.0$, while all 15 interior points yield $S < 0.5$,
confirming that the three-regime structure observed at the discrete operating
points of Table~\ref{tab:regime_inversion} is not a sampling artefact but a
robust feature of the full two-dimensional landscape.

\subsection{Lower-bound achievability: how close does Orion reach the physical minimum?}
\label{app:achievability}

The structural lower bound (Eq.~\ref{eq:lower_bound_regime} of the main text)
establishes that regime transitions are inevitable, but it does not directly
show that the bound is \emph{achievable}---i.e., that any orchestration policy
can drive $T_{\mathrm{total}}$ close to
$T_{\mathrm{mem}}^{\min} + T_{\mathrm{swap}}^{\min}$.
We quantify achievability by comparing two tightness ratios at each regime
boundary: (i)~the PyTorch baseline tightness
$\tau_{\mathrm{PT}} = T_{\mathrm{lb}} / T_{\mathrm{total}}^{\mathrm{PT}}$
(already reported in Table~\ref{tab:platform_constants}), and
(ii)~the Orion tightness
$\tau_{\mathrm{Or}} = T_{\mathrm{lb}} / T_{\mathrm{total}}^{\mathrm{Orion}}$,
measured at the boundary operating point
($R_C = \theta_C$ or $R_B = \theta_B$) with $n_{\mathrm{eff}} = 150$ windows.
Since $T_{\mathrm{lb}}$ is policy-independent and Orion reduces
$T_{\mathrm{total}}$, $\tau_{\mathrm{Or}} > \tau_{\mathrm{PT}}$ at all
boundary points; the residual gap $1 - \tau_{\mathrm{Or}}$ is the fraction
of $T_{\mathrm{total}}^{\mathrm{Orion}}$ not explained by the lower bound,
which is attributable to the excluded $T_{\mathrm{sync}}$ term.

synchronisation overhead, but unavoidable inter-layer coordination events

not algorithmic suboptimality.

(where $K$ is the number of unavoidable synchronisation events and $c_o$ the
per-event cost) would close the residual gap; we leave this to future work.

\section{Operational Definition and Measurement of Working-Set Size $W$}
\label{app:working_set}

\subsection{Formal definition}

For a transformer with $L$ layers, $H$ attention heads, head dimension $d_h$,
and model width $d_{\mathrm{model}}$, the \emph{active working-set size}
per inference step is:
\begin{equation}
W \;=\; W_{\mathrm{param}} + W_{\mathrm{act}} + W_{\mathrm{kv}},
\label{eq:W_def}
\end{equation}
with:
\begin{align}
W_{\mathrm{param}} &= |\theta| \cdot s_{\mathrm{dtype}}, \label{eq:Wparam}\\
W_{\mathrm{act}}   &= B \cdot \ell \cdot L \cdot 4 d_{\mathrm{model}}
                    \cdot s_{\mathrm{dtype}}, \label{eq:Wact}\\
W_{\mathrm{kv}}    &= 2 \cdot B \cdot L \cdot H \cdot d_h \cdot \ell
                    \cdot s_{\mathrm{dtype}}. \label{eq:Wkv}
\end{align}
$W_{\mathrm{act}}$ assumes standard forward-pass activation storage without
activation checkpointing; FlashAttention-based implementations reduce
$W_{\mathrm{act}}$ by up to $O(\sqrt{L})$ but do not affect $W_{\mathrm{param}}$
or $W_{\mathrm{kv}}$.

\subsection{Measurement methodology}

$W$ is measured at runtime by \textsc{Orion}'s allocator-level memory tracker,
which instruments PyTorch's caching allocator and records the high-water mark
of GPU-resident tensor bytes over a full prefill+decode cycle.

For BF16 Llama-3\,8B ($B{=}8$, $\ell{=}2048$):
\begin{align*}
W_{\mathrm{param}} &= 8.0 \times 10^9 \times 2 = 16.0\;\text{GB}, \\
W_{\mathrm{act}}   &= 8 \times 2048 \times 32 \times 4 \times 4096 \times 2
                    \approx 17.2\;\text{GB}, \\
W_{\mathrm{kv}}    &= 2 \times 8 \times 32 \times 32 \times 128 \times 2048
                    \times 2 \approx 8.6\;\text{GB}, \\
W               &\approx 41.8\;\text{GB}.
\end{align*}

With $C_{\mathrm{fast}} = 80\;\text{GB}$ (A100 HBM):
$R_C = 80/41.8 \approx 1.91$, well inside the coordination-dominated regime.
Reducing effective $C_{\mathrm{fast}}$ to 20\,GB (by pinning 60\,GB of weights
to host DRAM) gives $R_C \approx 0.48 < \theta_C$, entering the
capacity-limited regime---consistent with experimental observations.
